Androgenetic alopecia

Hair follicles that are genetically sensitive to androgens gradually become smaller. Each growth cycle produces thinner, shorter hair until some follicles stop producing visible hair.

Hair cycle shift

Normally, follicles rotate between growth (anagen), transition (catagen) and rest (telogen). In hereditary loss, anagen becomes shorter and telogen longer, leading to more shedding and less density.
